Brand Vault (Blockchain)

A cryptographically‑secured archive for authentic brand assets — binding ownership, provenance, and licensing to every file.

What the Brand Vault Is

The VISIVA® Brand Vault is a secure provenance system that verifies the authenticity and ownership of brand assets. Each critical asset (logos, master templates, sonic marks, brand films, guidelines) is fingerprinted, registered, and tracked with immutable records — ensuring your organisation always knows what is official, who owns it, where it came from, and how it may be used.

Authenticity

Every master asset is cryptographically hashed and matched to its original source.

Ownership

Clear, auditable record of IP ownership, rights, and transfer history.

Provenance

Trace the complete lifecycle — creation, revisions, approvals, and issuance.

Why the Brand Vault Matters

Counterfeit Prevention

Prevent unauthorised or edited assets from circulating as “official.”

Legal Confidence

Bind assets to trademarks, licenses, and contracts for defensible governance.

Enterprise Control

Know exactly which version is current and who is allowed to use it.

How the Brand Vault Works

The Vault combines cryptographic fingerprinting, immutable records, and governance metadata to authenticate brand assets and their permitted usage.

Fingerprinting

Each file (SVG, PNG, PDF, MP3/MP4, etc.) is hashed to create a unique fingerprint. Any change to the file produces a different hash, preventing silent edits.

Registration

The hash, issuer, timestamp, version, and metadata (licensing, region, expiry) are recorded and sealed. Official releases receive a verifiable certificate.

Verification

When an asset is used or uploaded, Guardian AI checks its hash and metadata against the Vault to confirm authenticity, rights, and current status.

What the Vault Stores

Master Assets

Primary logos, lockups, grid sets, core palettes, type tokens, sonic marks.

System Files

Brand guidelines, component libraries, motion rules, 3D/AR assets.

Certificates & Records

Authenticity certificates, issuance logs, approvals, transfers, and revocations.
